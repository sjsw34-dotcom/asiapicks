Jeju Island is one of those rare places that genuinely earns all the hype. Volcanic coastlines, emerald tea fields, turquoise beaches, and a food scene built around black pork and fresh haenyeo-caught seafood — it's a world unto itself, just a one-hour flight from Seoul. But here's the thing: *where* you stay on Jeju makes an enormous difference to your trip. The island is bigger than most first-timers expect, and the "best area" really depends on what kind of traveler you are. Beach lover? Culture seeker? Hiker? Party-goer? This guide breaks it all down so you can stop overthinking and start packing.

## Jeju City (Jeju-si) — Best for First-Timers and Easy Access

If it's your first visit to Jeju and you're not renting a car, **Jeju City is your home base**. The island's main hub sits on the northern coast, hosting the international airport, the ferry terminal, and the best concentration of restaurants, cafes, and shopping streets. It's not the most scenic part of the island, but it's incredibly convenient — and some excellent hotels are here.

### Top Picks in Jeju City

- **Lotte City Hotel Jeju** — A polished business-style hotel within walking distance of Dongmun Traditional Market. Rooms from around **$110/night**. Great buffet breakfast and a rooftop pool. *Address: 19 Doryeong-ro, Jeju-si.*
- **Jeju Shinhwa World Marriott Resort** — Technically on the west coast but easy to access from the city. Massive resort complex with pools, a casino, and theme parks. Rooms from **$180/night**.
- **Suites Hotel Jeju** — A solid mid-range option with modern rooms and a central location. From **$70/night**. Perfect if you want to keep costs down without sacrificing comfort.

**Getting around:** Buses run frequently from Jeju City to most attractions. Renting a car from the airport (from around **$35/day**) is highly recommended for flexibility — you can book rentals easily through Klook before you arrive.

## Seogwipo — Best for Nature Lovers and Honeymooners

Seogwipo, on the southern coast, is Jeju's second city and arguably its most *beautiful* urban area. Surrounded by waterfalls, lava cliffs, and the stunning Olle Trail network, it has a slower, more romantic pace than the north. The famous **Cheonjiyeon** and **Jeongbang** waterfalls are both within walking distance of the city center.

### Top Picks in Seogwipo

- **Aqua Planet Jeju & Haevichi Hotel** — A luxury resort right on the eastern edge of Seogwipo with direct beach access and an adjoining aquarium. Rooms from **$200/night**. Ideal for couples or families. *Address: 18 Haevichi-ro, Seogwipo-si.*
- **The Shilla Jeju** — One of the island's most iconic five-star properties perched above the ocean. Think infinity pools, spa facilities, and panoramic views of the southern coast. Rooms from **$280/night**. Worth every won for a special occasion.
- **Seogwipo KAL Hotel** — A classic mid-range option with sea views and a great central location. From **$95/night**. A reliable choice if The Shilla is out of budget.

Seogwipo is also the best jumping-off point for **Hallasan National Park** hikes — book guided hiking experiences through Klook if you want an English-speaking guide to lead you up the crater trail.

## Jungmun Resort Area — Best for Beach Resorts and Luxury Stays

If you picture a classic beach resort holiday with turquoise water, sun loungers, and cocktails at sunset, **Jungmun** is your spot. This planned resort district on the southwest coast is home to Jeju's best stretch of beach — **Jungmun Saekdal Beach** — along with a cluster of the island's most prestigious hotels.

### Top Picks in Jungmun

| Hotel | Style | Price From | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hyatt Regency Jeju | Luxury Resort | $220/night | Beach access, pools |
| Lotte Hotel Jeju | Deluxe Resort | $190/night | Families, facilities |
| Inter-Urban Hotel Jeju | Boutique | $130/night | Design-forward couples |
| Hotel Baume | Guesthouse-style | $80/night | Budget-conscious travelers |

The **Hyatt Regency Jeju** (*3039 Jungmun-gwangwang-ro, Seogwipo-si*) is the standout — it sits right above the beach, has multiple pools, and the breakfast spread is genuinely impressive. It's the kind of place you book on Agoda, see the price, wince slightly, then book it anyway because you deserve it.

Jungmun is also conveniently close to **Teddy Bear Museum**, **Jusangjeolli Cliff**, and the **Yeomiji Botanical Garden** — easy half-day excursions you can book through Klook if you want skip-the-queue entry.

## East Jeju (Seongsan & Udo Island Area) — Best for Scenic Escapes

The east coast of Jeju is *dramatically* different from the resort-heavy west and south. Here you'll find **Seongsan Ilchulbong** (Sunrise Peak), the iconic UNESCO-listed volcanic crater rising from the sea, plus the ferry port to the tiny, cycling-friendly **Udo Island**. The vibe here is peaceful, a little rustic, and achingly photogenic.

### Top Picks in East Jeju

- **Marriott Jeju Shinhwa World** *(for those driving east)* — If you have a car and want to explore the east coast from a luxury base, this works well for a split itinerary.
- **Seongsan Ilchulbong Guesthouse** — Dozens of small, clean guesthouses cluster around Seongsan Village. Expect to pay **$50–$80/night** for a private room with breakfast included. Search Agoda for the top-rated options — they fill up fast on weekends.
- **Haevichi Hotel & Resort** — Right on **Pyoseon Beach**, one of Jeju's most beautiful sandy shores on the southeast coast. Rooms from **$175/night**. The beach here is calmer and less crowded than Jungmun.

Staying in Seongsan means waking up at 5am to catch the sunrise from the crater summit without the tour bus crowds. It's the kind of early morning that resets you completely — bring a jacket, it's worth it.

## West Jeju (Hallim & Aewol) — Best for Café Culture and Slow Travel

The west coast has quietly become Jeju's coolest neighborhood. **Aewol** in particular is packed with Instagram-famous cafés perched on lava rock cliffs overlooking the sea — the most famous being **Cafe Monsant** and **Bonte Museum** (think minimalist Korean architecture meets ocean views). **Hallim** further south offers the famous **Manjanggul Lava Tube** and Hyeopjae Beach, one of Jeju's prettiest.

### Top Picks in West Jeju

- **Hotel Podo Jeju** — A stylish boutique hotel in Aewol surrounded by hydrangea fields. Rooms from **$140/night**. The kind of place design magazines photograph.
- **Jeju Ponds Hotel** — Modern, design-forward hotel near Hallim with gorgeous natural surrounds. From **$120/night**.
- **Villa Baume** — A collection of private villa-style accommodations near Hyeopjae Beach. Perfect for couples. From **$160/night**.

## Practical Tips for Booking Hotels in Jeju

Before you confirm your stay, a few things worth knowing:

1. **Book early for weekends and Korean holidays** — Jeju is Korea's most popular domestic destination. Chuseok (autumn harvest) and Seollal (Lunar New Year) periods see hotels sell out weeks in advance.
2. **Rent a car** — Jeju's public buses are functional but slow. A rental car (book through Klook or the airport counters) transforms your trip entirely and opens up the best coastal roads.
3. **Split your stay** — Consider two nights in Seogwipo/Jungmun and two nights in Seongsan or Aewol. You'll cover far more ground without exhausting yourself.
4. **Check for ocean views specifically** — Many Jeju hotels advertise "sea view" rooms that are actually partial or obstructed. Read Agoda reviews carefully and filter for confirmed ocean view rooms.
5. **Shoulder seasons are best** — April–June and September–October offer great weather, smaller crowds, and noticeably better hotel rates than July–August peak summer.
6. **Budget range guide:**
   - Budget: $50–$90/night (guesthouses, business hotels)
   - Mid-range: $100–$180/night (branded hotels, boutique stays)
   - Luxury: $200+/night (Shilla, Hyatt, resort properties)

Jeju rewards travelers who plan their base carefully. Whether you're sipping coffee on a lava-cliff terrace in Aewol, watching sunrise from Seongsan crater, or floating in a rooftop pool above Jungmun Beach — the right hotel in the right area makes all the difference.
