Phuket is one of those destinations that sounds simple on the surface — "just a beach island" — until you actually start planning your trip and realize the island is *enormous*. The west coast alone stretches across a dozen distinct beach zones, each with its own vibe, price range, and type of traveler. Stay in the wrong area and you might spend half your holiday in a taxi. Stay in the right one and everything — the beach, the bars, the boat tours — is right at your doorstep.

This guide breaks down the best hotels in Phuket by area so you can skip the guesswork and get straight to the good stuff.

## Patong Beach — For Party Lovers and First-Timers

Patong is Phuket's most famous — and most chaotic — beach area. It's loud, it's bright, it's full of tourist restaurants and tailor shops, and honestly? For many first-time visitors, it's exactly what they're looking for. Bangla Road, Patong's infamous nightlife strip, runs just two blocks from the beach. Everything is walkable, tuk-tuks are everywhere, and the energy is relentless.

**Best hotels in Patong:**

- **Novotel Phuket Vintage Park** — *Centrally located on Rat-U-Thit Road, doubles from ~$80/night.* A reliable mid-range option with multiple pools and a great central location. Easy walking distance to the beach and Bangla Road.
- **Amari Phuket** — *Perched on the northern headland of Patong, from ~$150/night.* Slightly removed from the chaos but with stunning ocean views and a quieter atmosphere. The clifftop infinity pool is a highlight.
- **Holiday Inn Resort Patong** — *Beachfront on Thawewong Road, from ~$110/night.* Solid family-friendly pick with direct beach access and multiple dining options.

**Best for:** Solo travelers, groups of friends, first-timers who want convenience over tranquility.

## Kata and Karon — The Sweet Spot for Most Travelers

If someone asks me where to stay in Phuket and I don't know their vibe yet, I say Kata or Karon. These twin beach areas sit about 20 minutes south of Patong and hit a perfect balance: decent nightlife without the sensory overload, great beaches, and a range of hotels from budget guesthouses to proper luxury resorts.

Kata Beach is slightly more boutique and laid-back; Karon is longer and better for families. Both have a good spread of restaurants and shops along the main road.

**Best hotels in Kata and Karon:**

- **Katathani Phuket Beach Resort** — *On the quieter southern end of Kata Noi Beach, from ~$130/night.* One of the best-value beachfront resorts on the island. Multiple pools, a spa, and direct access to one of Phuket's most beautiful small beaches.
- **Centara Grand Beach Resort Phuket** — *On Karon Beach, from ~$160/night.* A large, well-run resort with a waterpark-style pool complex — excellent for families or couples who like a resort-within-a-resort feel.
- **The Shore at Katathani** — *Adults-only villas on Kata Noi, from ~$350/night.* If you want pure luxury in the Kata area, these private pool villas are stunning.

**Best for:** Couples, families, repeat visitors, travelers wanting a balance of beach life and local atmosphere.

## Kamala and Surin — Where Boutique Meets Upscale

Head north of Patong past the headland and you'll reach Kamala Beach — calmer, less crowded, and home to a growing collection of quality boutique hotels. Further north, Surin Beach is where Phuket's moneyed crowd tends to congregate, with a string of stylish beach clubs and high-end restaurants lining the road.

**Best hotels in Kamala and Surin:**

- **Hyatt Regency Phuket Resort** — *Kamala Beach hillside, from ~$200/night.* A genuinely beautiful resort with cascading pools and a peaceful atmosphere. The shuttle to the beach is frequent and easy.
- **Trisara Resort** — *Private cove near Surin, from ~$600/night.* One of Phuket's most exclusive properties. Think private pool villas, personal butler service, and a seafood restaurant perched over the water.
- **Catch Beach Club Residences** — *On Surin Beach Road, from ~$180/night.* Stylish boutique-style rooms attached to one of Phuket's most popular beach clubs. Perfect if you love the beach club lifestyle.

**Best for:** Honeymooners, luxury travelers, those who want upscale without feeling touristy.

## Rawai and Nai Harn — For Long-Stay Travelers and Off-the-Beaten-Path Seekers

The southern tip of Phuket is a different world. Rawai is where a lot of expats and long-stay travelers base themselves — it's less polished than the west coast beaches but has a genuinely local feel, great seafood markets, and easy access to offshore islands. Nai Harn Beach, just around the headland, is arguably the most beautiful beach on the island: a wide bay framed by green hills, with far fewer crowds than Kata or Karon.

**Best hotels in Rawai and Nai Harn:**

- **The Nai Harn** — *Overlooks Nai Harn Lake and Beach, from ~$200/night.* Consistently rated one of Phuket's top hotels. The rooftop pool bar with lake views is extraordinary, and the beach is a 3-minute walk.
- **Vijitt Resort Phuket** — *On Friendship Beach in Rawai, from ~$110/night.* Thai-style villa resort with a boutique feel. Quiet, beautiful grounds, and a great base for exploring the south.

**Best for:** Repeat visitors, digital nomads, travelers who prefer authentic over touristy.

## Phuket Town — For Culture, Design, and Budget Value

People often overlook Phuket Town as a base, but it's genuinely underrated. The Sino-Portuguese Old Town is charming, full of street art and excellent local restaurants, and accommodation here tends to be significantly cheaper than on the beach. The downside? You'll need a rental scooter or regular Grab rides to reach the beaches (~20-30 minutes to Patong).

**Best hotels in Phuket Town:**

- **The Memory at On On Hotel** — *On Phang Nga Road in Old Town, from ~$35/night.* Historic boutique hotel that was actually featured in *The Beach* (the film). Bags of character at a budget-friendly price.
- **SINO Imperial Style Hotel** — *Old Town, from ~$55/night.* Beautifully restored Sino-Portuguese building with stylish rooms and a great location for walking the heritage streets.

**Best for:** Budget-conscious travelers, design and culture lovers, those combining Phuket with nearby day trips.

## Practical Tips for Booking Your Phuket Hotel

| Area | Best For | Avg. Price/Night | Distance to Airport |
|------|----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Patong | Nightlife, convenience | $80–$150 | 40 min |
| Kata/Karon | Families, couples | $100–$160 | 50 min |
| Kamala/Surin | Luxury, boutique | $150–$600+ | 30 min |
| Rawai/Nai Harn | Local feel, peace | $100–$200 | 60 min |
| Phuket Town | Budget, culture | $30–$80 | 25 min |

A few things worth knowing before you book:

- **Book on Agoda** for Phuket — the deals are genuinely better than most platforms, especially for Thai properties, and the cancellation policies tend to be flexible.
- **Peak season runs November to April.** Prices can double during Christmas and New Year. Book at least 2-3 months ahead for any decent property during this window.
- **Shoulder season (May–June) is underrated.** Occasional rain but far fewer crowds, much lower rates, and the sea is usually still swimmable.
- **Don't underestimate transport.** Phuket is about 50km from top to bottom. If your hotel isn't near a beach or you plan to explore multiple areas, budget for Grab rides or rent a scooter (~$7-10/day).
- **Look for free airport transfers.** Many mid-range and upscale hotels offer these — always worth asking when you book.
- **For island day trips** (Phi Phi, James Bond Island, Phang Nga Bay), it's worth booking through Klook in advance — you'll often get better pricing and can pick up from your hotel area.
