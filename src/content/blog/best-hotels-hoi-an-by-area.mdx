Hoi An might just be the most photogenic town in all of Southeast Asia — and choosing *where* to stay here makes a bigger difference than almost anywhere else in Vietnam. The Ancient Town has a strict no-new-construction rule, An Bang Beach is a laid-back surfer hangout, and the countryside rice paddies sit somewhere beautifully in between. Get the location wrong and you'll spend half your trip in a taxi. Get it right, and you'll be sipping Vietnamese coffee on your guesthouse balcony while lanterns drift past on the Thu Bon River. This guide breaks it all down by area so you can find your perfect Hoi An base in 2025.

## Understanding Hoi An's Key Neighborhoods

Before you book, it helps to know what you're actually choosing between. Hoi An isn't a sprawling city — it's compact and manageable — but each pocket has a genuinely different vibe.

| Area | Best For | Avg. Distance to Ancient Town | Price Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ancient Town | History lovers, romantics | Walking distance | $80–$300+/night |
| Cam Nam Island | Budget travelers, locals | 5–10 min bike ride | $25–$80/night |
| An Bang Beach | Beach seekers, slow travelers | 20–25 min bike | $40–$250/night |
| Cua Dai Beach | Resort families, splurgers | 25–30 min bike | $80–$400+/night |
| Countryside / Tra Que | Eco-travelers, couples | 15–20 min bike | $50–$180/night |

Most visitors base themselves near the Ancient Town or at the beach, then rent a bicycle ($2–3/day) or motorbike ($6–8/day) to get around. It's genuinely that easy.

## Ancient Town & Surrounds: Where the Magic Happens

If this is your first time in Hoi An, **staying inside or right next to the Ancient Town** is hard to argue against. You wake up to the smell of *bánh mì* from the street carts below, you can walk to the Japanese Covered Bridge in five minutes, and the whole lantern-lit evening scene is literally on your doorstep.

The trade-off? It gets noisy at night (especially on weekends and Full Moon Festival nights), motorbikes are banned inside the core after certain hours, and quality hotels inside the protected zone are limited by heritage laws.

### Top Picks: Ancient Town Area

- **Anantara Hoi An Resort** — 1 Pham Hong Thai St. One of the most elegant properties right on the river, with beautifully restored colonial architecture, a pool, and a spa. Rates from **$180–$260/night**. Book early on Agoda for the best deals, especially in high season (February–April).

- **Vinh Hung Riverside Resort & Spa** — 1 Nguyen Phuc Chu St. A mid-range gem with a rooftop pool overlooking the river. Expect to pay around **$70–$110/night** depending on the season. Great staff, genuinely warm service.

- **La Siesta Hoi An Resort & Spa** — 132 Hung Vuong St. Boutique-style with a long pool, lovely garden, and an outstanding breakfast spread. Rates hover around **$90–$140/night**. This is a crowd favorite for good reason.

- **Hoi An Historic Hotel** — 10 Tran Hung Dao St. A reliable three-star option in a prime location. Rooms from **$45–$75/night**, making it one of the better value picks this close to the action.

**Pro tip:** Look for properties on or just off Nguyen Thai Hoc and Tran Phu streets — you're in the thick of it without being directly above a bar strip.

## An Bang Beach: Hoi An's Coolest Coastal Village

About 4km from the Ancient Town, **An Bang Beach** has become the go-to spot for travelers who want a beach base without the corporate resort feel. It's lined with low-key beach bars, excellent seafood shacks, and a handful of really charming boutique guesthouses. The vibe is relaxed, creative, and genuinely local — think sun loungers, cold *bia hơi*, and sunsets that go on forever.

The beach itself is clean and swimmable from roughly February through September. Outside of that window, swells pick up and jellyfish can be an issue — plan accordingly.

### Top Picks: An Bang Beach

- **An Bang Seaside Village** — A collection of self-contained bungalows set in lush gardens just steps from the sand. Rates from **$80–$130/night**. Perfect for couples who want privacy with easy beach access.

- **Hoi An Beach Resort** — Tran Hung Dao St, An Bang. A well-managed mid-range property with a solid pool, clean rooms, and helpful staff. Rooms from **$60–$100/night**. Agoda frequently has flash deals here.

- **Sandy Feet Vietnam** — Tiny, personal, and beloved. Run by an Australian-Vietnamese couple, this guesthouse has a cult following for its homey atmosphere and proximity to the beach. Rates around **$35–$55/night** — book directly for the best rate.

## Cua Dai Beach: Upscale Resorts & Family Stays

If An Bang is the indie record store, **Cua Dai** is the boutique hotel strip — polished, amenity-rich, and ideal for travelers who want a full resort experience with direct beach access. It's about 5km from the Ancient Town, and most resorts here offer free shuttle service into town, which takes the stress out of logistics.

Note that Cua Dai has seen some significant beach erosion over the years, so check recent reviews before booking a specific beachfront property — the situation varies considerably from resort to resort.

### Top Picks: Cua Dai Beach

- **Four Seasons Resort The Nam Hai** — Hamlet 1, Dien Duong Village. The undisputed top-end option in the entire Hoi An area. Three pool tiers descending to a private beach, exceptional dining, and villas starting at **$400–$700+/night**. Worth every dong for a special occasion.

- **Sunrise Premium Resort Hoi An** — Au Co St, Cua Dai. A beautifully designed property with multiple pools and strong service. Rates from **$120–$200/night**. Great for families or anyone who wants resort facilities without the Four Seasons price tag.

- **Victoria Hoi An Beach Resort & Spa** — Cua Dai Beach. Colonial French-inspired architecture, a gorgeous pool, and solid food and beverage options. Rates from **$110–$180/night**.

## Cam Nam Island & Countryside: The Local Alternative

**Cam Nam Island** sits just across the river from the Ancient Town — you can literally walk over the footbridge in two minutes — but it feels worlds quieter. This is where you'll find small family-run guesthouses, local restaurants, and almost no tourists wandering past your window. Budget travelers love it.

Further out, the **Tra Que Vegetable Village area** offers a handful of eco-lodges and boutique farm stays that are genuinely special. Waking up to misty rice paddies and cycling into town for breakfast is a very different Hoi An experience — and an increasingly popular one.

- **Cam Nam Village Homestay** — From **$20–$40/night**. Basic but clean, with warm hosts and a genuinely local feel.
- **Mia Eco Resort** — Tra Que area. Beautifully designed eco-lodges surrounded by herb gardens. Rates from **$85–$150/night**. If you're booking activities on Klook, the Tra Que cooking class pairs perfectly with a stay here.

## Practical Tips for Booking Hotels in Hoi An

A few things that will save you hassle (and money):

1. **Book early for peak season** — February through April and late July through August fill up fast. The Full Moon Festival months are especially popular; some properties sell out 3–4 months ahead.

2. **Ask about free bike rentals** — Many mid-range and boutique hotels offer complimentary bicycles. In Hoi An, that's genuinely worth $15–20 per day in savings.

3. **Check shuttle policies** — If you're staying at Cua Dai or An Bang, confirm whether the hotel runs a free tuk-tuk or van into the Ancient Town. Most decent properties do, typically a few times daily.

4. **Avoid booking the cheapest rooms on the main bar streets** — Nguyen Hoang St gets very loud on weekends. If you're a light sleeper, filter for properties on side streets or ask specifically about room placement.

5. **Agoda often beats other platforms for Vietnam properties** — Particularly for smaller boutique guesthouses that have exclusive deals. It's worth cross-checking before you commit.

6. **The shoulder season sweet spot is May–June** — Prices drop 20–40%, crowds thin out, and the weather is still very manageable. Some of the best deals on boutique hotels appear during this window.

7. **Always read reviews from the past 6 months** — Ownership and management at smaller guesthouses can change, and a property that was fantastic in 2023 might have declined. Recent reviews are your best signal.

## Final Verdict: Which Area Is Right for You?

- **First-time visitor who wants the full Hoi An experience?** → Stay in or adjacent to the **Ancient Town**.
- **Beach lover who wants a relaxed, non-resort vibe?** → **An Bang Beach** is your people.
- **Traveling with family or celebrating something special?** → **Cua Dai** resorts deliver the amenities.
- **Budget-conscious or craving a local experience?** → **Cam Nam Island** or the **countryside** will surprise you.

Hoi An is small enough that no area locks you out of anything — it's all about what you want to wake up to every morning. Choose that first, then let everything else fall into place.
